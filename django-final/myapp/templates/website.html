Social housing in Queensland, Australia

Social housing in Queensland, Australia, is either provided by the government, or by non-profit organizations. This page provides the following information:

● Characteristics of social housing in different Local Government Areas (LGA) in Queensland.

● A geographic map of Queensland indicating the economic disadvantage per LGA in quintiles (highest 20% zu lowest 20%).

● A plot showing the average number of vacancy days by property size for the most disadvantaged LGA.

The data on social housing provides great insights into existing gaps of demand and supply of social housing. One of the main findings is that in areas of economic disadvantage, less money is spent on the maintenance of social housing. Also, not surprisingly, the demand is higher in areas of higher economic disadvantage. The government should focus on providing better supply in such areas and adjust the supply to the demand considering the property size (number of bedrooms).

Background Information:

Definition of terms:

Loval Government Area (LGA): An LGA is a defined geographic area in the Australian state of Queensland, it can be understood as a neighborhood.

Economic Disadvantage Index: This index indicates the economic wealth per LGA and is shown in Quintiles. For each LGA, the index gives us the quintile were is stands in comparison with all LGAs in Queensland.
